Junior Cycle Maths

New features of this specification include learning outcomes across a unifying strand which permeate all the other strands:
Number, Geometry and trigonometry, Algebra and functions and Statistics and probability. In each strand of the specification the
learning outcomes associated with a particular mathematical idea have been grouped together thus supporting the conception of
mathematics as an interconnected body of ideas and reasoning processes that students explore collaboratively with their teachers
and their peers. In addition, the grouping makes explicit the cumulative nature of mathematics. There are also new assessments
which offers students a chance to demonstrate their achievement as creators of mathematical reports. Teachers, students and
parents will be able to check progress by logging in to examples of student work where they will see the range of annotated
examples of work that students in junior cycle Mathematics create and see the learning outcomes in action.
